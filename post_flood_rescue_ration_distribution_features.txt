POST-FLOOD RESCUE AND RATION DISTRIBUTION MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
Detailed Project Features Document
Member 4 Component

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

The Post-Flood Rescue and Ration Distribution Management System is the final decision-support component of the overall flood disaster management solution. This component receives outputs from the other group members and uses them to support post-flood rescue planning, ration distribution, camp prioritization, relief item prioritization, and safe route planning.

The corrected system flow is based on the idea that refugee camps must be located inside safe zones. Therefore, the system does not directly identify camps in flood-affected areas. Instead, it first uses the combined flood-related map to identify safe zones, then identifies or manages camps within those safe zones, and finally prioritizes camps and relief distribution activities.

Main purpose:
To help disaster management authorities decide:
- Which safe-zone camps need urgent support
- What relief items are required for each camp
- Which camps should be served first
- Which safe route should be used for rescue and ration distribution

2. CORRECTED COMPONENT FLOW

Flood Risk Map + Drain Water Level Map
                +
      Disease Detection Results
                ↓
       Safe Zone Identification
                ↓
     Camp Identification in Safe Zones
                ↓
        Camp Need Analysis
 (population, available resources, health risk)
                ↓
      Camp Priority Prediction
         (High / Medium / Low)
                ↓
      Relief Item Prioritization
 (food, water, medicine, sanitary items)
                ↓
        Safe Route / Distribution Planning
                ↓
 Post-Flood Rescue and Ration Distribution Output

3. INPUTS FROM OTHER COMPONENTS

3.1 Input from Member 1 and Member 2

Member 1 and Member 2 together generate a combined flood-related map.

This map includes:
- Flood risk areas
- Flood severity levels
- Drain water level conditions
- Flood-affected roads or zones
- Safe areas suitable for temporary camps
- Unsafe areas that should be avoided during rescue and distribution

How this input is used in Member 4 component:
- To identify safe zones
- To avoid flood-affected or high-risk locations
- To select camps located only inside safe zones
- To support route planning by avoiding flooded roads
- To decide which areas are suitable for distribution operations

3.2 Input from Member 3

Member 3 provides disease detection results.

Disease detection results may include:
- Disease risk level in a camp or nearby area
- Possible outbreak alerts
- Health risk indicators
- Medicine urgency level
- Sanitary requirement level

How this input is used in Member 4 component:
- To include health risk in camp priority prediction
- To increase priority for camps with high disease risk
- To prioritize medicine and sanitary items
- To support healthcare-based relief planning

4. MAIN OUTPUT OF MEMBER 4 COMPONENT

The main output of this component is:

Prioritized rescue and ration distribution decision support output.

This output includes:
- Priority camps located inside safe zones
- Camp priority level: High, Medium, or Low
- Required relief item list for each camp
- Item priority level for food, water, medicine, and sanitary items
- Safe routes for rescue teams and ration delivery vehicles
- Response order showing which camp should be served first
- Final distribution plan for disaster management authorities

9. MACHINE LEARNING MODEL FEATURES

Recommended model:
- XGBoost Classifier
or
- Random Forest Classifier
or
- Decision Tree Classifier for simple implementation

Best recommendation:
Use XGBoost or Random Forest for camp priority classification.

9.1 Model Purpose

The model predicts the priority level of each camp.

Prediction classes:
- High priority
- Medium priority
- Low priority

9.2 Model Inputs / Features

Possible model features:
- population
- children_count
- elderly_count
- food_available
- water_available
- medicine_available
- sanitary_available
- disease_risk_level
- flood_severity_near_area
- distance_from_distribution_center
- road_access_score
- camp_capacity
- current_occupancy_rate

9.3 Feature Explanation

population:
Higher population means more urgent need.

children_count:
More children may increase priority because they are vulnerable.

elderly_count:
More elderly people may increase priority.

food_available:
Low food availability increases priority.

water_available:
Low water availability increases priority.

medicine_available:
Low medicine availability increases priority, especially with disease risk.

sanitary_available:
Low sanitary item availability increases disease-related risk.

disease_risk_level:
High disease risk increases camp urgency and medicine priority.

flood_severity_near_area:
High flood severity near the camp or access route increases rescue urgency.

distance_from_distribution_center:
Very far camps may need early planning.

road_access_score:
Poor road access may require careful route planning.

9.4 Model Output

The model should output:
- priority_level: High / Medium / Low
- priority_score: numeric score
- confidence_score: model confidence

10. RULE-BASED RELIEF ITEM PRIORITIZATION

This part decides which items are needed most.

10.1 Food Priority Rules

Food priority becomes High if:
- Population is high
- Food availability is low
- Food stock is not enough for the next 24 to 48 hours

Food priority becomes Medium if:
- Food stock is limited but not critical

Food priority becomes Low if:
- Food stock is enough for the camp

10.2 Water Priority Rules

Water priority becomes High if:
- Water availability is low
- Population is high
- Safe drinking water is unavailable

Water is usually one of the highest priority resources after floods.

10.3 Medicine Priority Rules

Medicine priority becomes High if:
- Disease risk is high
- Medicine availability is low
- Elderly or children count is high
- Disease detection result shows outbreak risk

10.4 Sanitary Item Priority Rules

Sanitary priority becomes High if:
- Disease risk is high
- Sanitary item availability is low
- Camp population is high
- Camp is overcrowded

10.5 Example Rule-Based Output

For Camp A:
- Food priority: High
- Water priority: High
- Medicine priority: Medium
- Sanitary priority: High

Suggested item plan:
- Food packs: 500
- Water bottles: 1000
- Medicine kits: 50
- Sanitary kits: 300

11. SAFE ROUTE PLANNING FEATURES

Safe route planning is a major part of this component.

11.1 Purpose

To find the safest and most efficient route from a distribution center to a selected camp located inside a safe zone.

11.2 Route Inputs

Route planning requires:
- Camp location
- Distribution center location
- Road network
- Flood-affected roads
- Blocked roads
- Drain water level data
- Flood severity map
- Safe zone boundaries

11.3 Route Output

The route planning module should output:
- Safe route path
- Distance
- Estimated travel time
- Safety score
- Alternative route if available
- Route warning if no safe route is found

11.4 Safe Route Conditions

A route is safe if:
- It does not pass through high flood-risk areas
- It does not pass through roads with high drain water levels
- It avoids blocked roads
- It avoids disease-risk hotspots if necessary
- It connects to the camp inside the safe zone
- It is suitable for rescue or delivery vehicles

11.5 Route Algorithm Options

Dijkstra Algorithm:
- Good for finding shortest path
- Works well with weighted road networks

A* Algorithm:
- Faster for map-based route planning
- Uses distance estimation to improve performance

Weighted Route Method:
Each road segment can be given a weight.

Example road weight factors:
- Distance
- Flood risk level
- Water level
- Road blockage
- Travel time
- Road safety score

Example:
Total route weight = distance weight + flood risk penalty + water level penalty + blockage penalty

The route with the lowest total weight can be selected as the safest route.

17. FINAL PROJECT SUMMARY

The Post-Flood Rescue and Ration Distribution Management System is designed to support post-flood disaster response by using outputs from other components and generating actionable decisions. The system first uses the combined flood risk and drain water level map to identify safe zones. Camps located inside these safe zones are then analyzed using population, available resources, and disease risk information. A machine learning model predicts the priority level of each camp, while a rule-based mechanism identifies the most urgent relief items. Finally, a route planning module recommends safe routes for rescue and ration delivery.

This component produces a complete decision-support output including priority camps, required items, safe delivery routes, and response order. It improves coordination, reduces manual decision-making, and supports faster and more effective post-flood rescue and ration distribution.
